A big warm welcome from Birmingham! The geographical heart of England, Birmingham began life as a sixth- century Anglo-Saxon village. Today, much of this city of over a million dates back to post-WWII redevelopment in the 1950s and 60s. We are also known as England's second city after London, and the city of a thousand trades. Birmingham in the West Midlands, was once a manufacturing powerhouse and continues to displays the results of its amazing growth during the Industrial Revolution. Cutting edge museums and galleries, innovative theatres and excellent shopping have contributed to Birmingham's appeal as a weekend break destination. Don't miss the Balti Triangle, home to the Pakistani food in the UK, the hopping bars and cafes of Gas Street Basin, or the National Sea Life Centre. So, whether you are a visitor or a local, there is so much for you to do and explore.

Find a Place You Love and Book Your Stay

City Centre

Birmingham's city centre is the best area to stay for visitors. It's home to popular tourist attractions such as the Bullring shopping centre and the Library of Birmingham, along with shopping options and a diverse range of restaurants, cafes, and bars.

Jewellery Quater

The Jewellery Quarter is a unique area located in the heart of Birmingham and is an excellent area to stay in for those who love history, culture, and art. The area is known for its stunning architecture, which features elegant Georgian and Victorian buildings. It is also home to over 100 jewellery stores and workshops, making it a perfect location for jewellery lovers.
